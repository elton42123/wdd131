Madagascar, the world's fourth-largest island, is a biodiversity hotspot located off the coast of East Africa. Known as the "eighth continent" due to its unique wildlife, Madagascar is home to species found nowhere else on Earth, including lemurs, fossas, and over 1,000 species of orchids.

The island's diverse landscapes range from tropical rainforests to arid deserts, making it a fascinating place with rich cultural heritage and natural wonders that continue to amaze researchers and visitors alike.
